Research Summary

The types of known matter only comprise 4.9% of the mass-energy density of our universe, whereas 25.9% is comprised of an unknown form of invisible matter called "dark matter (DM)" that is known only to interact gravitationally. Current evidence for the existence of dark matter comes from multiple sources including gravitational lensing data, rotational curves of galaxies, stars, and galactic clusters, and features of the cosmic microwave background radiation (CMB). Current attempts to produce dark matter at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC)’ at CERN and to measure the interactions of the dark matter in our own Milky Way galactic halo in detectors such as XENON1T and DAMIC have not found evidence for weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) that are predicted by supersymmetry to naturally have masses around 100 GeV. This lack of any direct information on how dark matter may couple to the standard model (SM) is the biggest question of the current century and has led to theoretical developments of new forms of dark matter such as hidden dark matter that interacts with the standard model through mixing with standard model force carriers. I wish to do research to solve this darkmatter mystry using dark matter detection through space based probes like WMAP, PLANCK and finding weak interaction of dark matter particles through ground based detectors.

Research Projects (ongoing)

  • Magnetism and spin-orbit interaction at complex oxide interfaces for technology: An ab initio investigation,

    with Dr. Nirmal Ganguli, IISER, Bhopal

    Hetero-interfaces between complex oxide insulators have unique properties that are not observed in conventional electronic devices. In particular, high density two dimensional electron gases (2DEGs) can be generated at the epitaxial interface of two perovskite-insulators, e.g., between LaAlO3 (LAO) and SrTiO3 (STO) crystals. As devices are scaled down to atomic dimensions, transistors will require extremely high charge densities. Silicon devices typically have two-dimensional electron gas densities of around 1012 cm−2; however, the 2DEG at the interface of oxide hetero-structures such as LAO/STO or Al2O3/STO has charge densities as high as >1013 cm−2 at the nanometer scale. In this project we are studying different complex interfaces using density functional theory.

Research Projects

Signature of Dark Matter at the center of Milky Way.

Avishek Singh and Dr. Rajib Saha, IISER, Bhopal
Master's thesis

Abstract

In this Project, we began with implementation of Iterative Linear Combination (ILC) on WMAP maps in modified way and Metropolis-Hasting technique of Markov Chain Monte Carlo simulation to find the“Haze/Bubble" signals at microwave wavelength in the center of our galaxy (Milky Way) from WMAP data and also to estimate the correct spectral index for “Haze" signals & synchrotron signals. The Haze is a distinct component of diffuse galactic emission, centered on the Galactic center and extends from 350 to 500 in Galactic latitude and from 150 to 200 in longitude. By using Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe (WMAP), we are able to filter these “Haze" signals and able to provide correct spectral index for these “Haze" signals. The modified Iterative linear Combination (ILC) of WMAP provided spectral index of these “Haze" signals, βDM = −2.56 ± 0.01 and Metropolis-Hasting technique of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulation provided spectral index of these “Haze" signals, βDM = −2.52 ± 0.01, which matches with the spectral index reported in many papers. As accompanying results of the method we also reconstructed Synchrotron Emission maps and estimated spectral index. only Iterative linear Combination (ILC) provided “synchrotron" spectral index, βs = −3.10 ± 0.01 and the implementation of Metropolis-Hasting technique of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulation provided “synchrotron" spectral index, βs = −2.90 ± 0.01. The Metropolis-Hasting technique of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulation as implemented in this work has interesting property that it can sample as many parameters as you want. We argue that, our method of “Haze" signal detection and spectral index determination is a maximum likelihood method.

Construction Of Foreground Emission Components.

Avishek Singh and Dr. Rajib Saha

Abstract

The main Idea of this project was to study the properties of cosmic foreground emissions and develop an method to separate these foreground emissions from CMB data provided WMAP and PLANK missions. We began with study of foreground emission properties by plotting them with respect to spectral index and frequency. Latter on we used linear combination of maps provided by WMAP and PLANK and tried to separate Synchrotron emission, Free-Free Emission and Thermal Dust emission from maps. We found Synchrotron Emission at spectral index -3.10, Free-free Emission at spectral index -2.19 and Thermal Dust Emission at spectral index 1.9.

Galaxy Interactions.,

Avishek Singh and Dr. Rajib Saha, IISER, Bhopal

Abstract

The central idea of the project, was to take models of two galaxies and try to study of their interaction. We started with the study of morphology of different galaxies. we used equation of motion for the galaxies and solved them using Runge-Kutta fourth order. This model shows how on galaxy can move under the influence of other galaxies. Though we didn’t added different parameters but we used sufficient parameters to illustrate a simple model.
